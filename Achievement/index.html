Finalists: Smart India Hackathon 2020 Software edition

Mayank Srivastava, Srivatsan M, Robin Singh, Abhishek Satayanarayanan, Aishika Das and Ishan Jain

The team designed a web application in Flask to predict the quality of air in a city. This was calculated by entering the concentrations of SO2 and NO2 along with SPM(Suspended Particulate Participation) and RPM.... A graph of the AQI value and years in the city shows the yearly rise of AQI and compares it with those of other cities to help the government in analysing the cause. the hotspots of Air Pollution with AQI crossing 200 would be displayed on a map classifying Urban and Rural Hotspots.

AAKRUTI ZONAL

Heading swiftly into Aakruti Zonal with the mechanism of converting Kinetic Energy of wind, flowing relative to a body, into electric energy, “Triborotor”, was selected in the top 10 of the regional round of the competition, in 2019. Triborotor consists of a rotor and a ...triboelectric generator which converts friction of two bodies moving relative to each other, into electricity. The rotor rotates and turns the yolk and shaw mechanism which is connected to the triboelectric generator. This set-up can be mounted on large vehicles such as trucks or trains, to generate energy which could be enough to balance out everyday energy requirements.

SOLIDWORKS xDesign India Challenge

Crossing the 1.3 million mark, India ranks as the second-most populous country in the world, securing 65% workforce, “Congestion” is a major cause for scrutiny. How about revolutionizing and transforming India’s public transportation? Carrying such thoughts in mind,... IIT Bombay Operation Rahat

Being a partaker in one of Asia’s largest tech fest hosted by IIT Bombay, Team SCRO was one of the contestants in the Drone Making Challenge. Visualizing and Implementing the framework which was entirely 3D printed, the printing was done by using ABS(Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)... which efficiently handles the heat dissipated from the motors while the drone maneuvers. Widely appreciated, the elephant in the room had many benefits such as the image processing camera, through which the video could be assessed on mobile phones or at ground stations. Foldable, lightweight, and most importantly economical, the proposition presented by the team grabbed everybody’s attention. Preserved as one of the proudest moments for Team SCRO to showcase their innovation at an extravagant technology fest, the experience was unmatchable!
